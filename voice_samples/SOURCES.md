# Reference voice sources

All clips in this directory are 5–15 s single-voice samples used as reference audio for Chatterbox (voice-cloning TTS). Every clip must be attributable to a public-domain or Creative-Commons source.

## Current clips

- **Source**: *The Great Gatsby (Version 5, Dramatic Reading)*, LibriVox 2025
- **Reader (in role)**: Tomas Peter as Jay Gatsby
- **File**: `greatgatsby_05_fitzgerald.mp3`
- **URL**: https://archive.org/download/greatgatsby5_2510_librivox/greatgatsby_05_fitzgerald.mp3

### `narrator_suzy_ref.wav` — Suzy Jackson (commercial audiobook sample)

- **Source**: commercial audiobook sample of Suzy Jackson's narration of Brandon Sanderson's Skyward series, fetched from a YouTube audio-only stream (Opus 150 kbps).
- **Timestamps**: 2:47.5–3:01.5 (14.0 s)
- **Why this clip**: Used to voice **narrator** in *Hyperthief*. Suzy Jackson is the canonical audiobook narrator for the Skyward series; the user specifically requested her voice so the Hyperthief audiobook matches the rest of the series sonically.
- **Legal caveat**: **This clip is NOT public domain.** Suzy Jackson is a professional voice actor; her commercial audiobook recordings are copyrighted by the publisher and her performance is protected by right-of-publicity. Using her voice as a Chatterbox reference to clone and generate new speech is legally gray at best (voice-identity-rights cases are unsettled as of 2026).
- **Scope of use**: this clip is included **for personal-consumption rendering only**, at the user's explicit request and with the user's acceptance of the risk. The cloned output (`out/Hyperthief.m4b`) must not be distributed, shared, or published. `voice_samples/*.wav` is gitignored so the clip does not land in the public repo. If this voice-sample library is ever opened up (public release, UI share feature, etc.), this clip must be removed first.
- **How this differs from the Gatsby V5 clips**: those clips are LibriVox dramatic readings — PD text + PD-release performance — and the cloned output is equally free to distribute. Suzy Jackson's clip is a one-off personal-use exception documented here so future maintainers understand the distinction.

## Legal status

*The Great Gatsby* (F. Scott Fitzgerald, 1925) entered the US public domain on 1 January 2021. LibriVox recordings are released into the public domain by their readers. Both conditions are satisfied: the underlying text and the recording performance are both PD. No attribution is legally required, but it is given here for auditability.

## Extraction commands

For reproducibility:

```bash
SRC5=voice_samples/_librivox_src/greatgatsby_05.mp3
ffmpeg -y -ss 1083.8 -to 1091.7 -i "$SRC5" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/gatsby_ref.wav
ffmpeg -y -ss 1343.5 -to 1352.8 -i "$SRC5" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/daisy_ref.wav

SRC1=voice_samples/_librivox_src/greatgatsby_01.mp3
SRC4=voice_samples/_librivox_src/greatgatsby_04.mp3
SRC7=voice_samples/_librivox_src/greatgatsby_07.mp3
ffmpeg -y -ss 17.6     -to 28.4    -i "$SRC1" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/rig_ref.wav
ffmpeg -y -ss 3146.68  -to 3151.96 -i "$SRC7" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/jorgen_ref.wav
ffmpeg -y -ss 1505.0   -to 1510.6  -i "$SRC5" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/fm_ref.wav
ffmpeg -y -ss 1612.96  -to 1622.0  -i "$SRC4" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/alanik_ref.wav
ffmpeg -y -ss 1607.76  -to 1613.84 -i "$SRC5" -ac 1 -ar 24000 voice_samples/nedd_ref.wav
```

## How timestamps were chosen

Rather than listening through the 29-minute chapter manually, the reunion-scene content was located via `faster-whisper` transcription with word-level timestamps. A short script (`/tmp/find_clips.py` during development) matched known dialogue lines from our `script.json` ("Five years next November", "It's stopped", etc.) against the Whisper output and returned their time ranges. Contextual passages were then inspected to find sustained single-voice moments. This kept sourcing auditable and reproducible — the same Whisper pass against a different LibriVox reading would surface equivalent timestamps.
